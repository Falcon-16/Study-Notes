Simplifying Surds

One of the key things to be able to do with surds, is being able to simplify them.
Let's look at simplifying

  3. Find the largest square that will factorise into
  4. Factorise the surd
  5. Separate the surds
  6. Simplify
    That's it!

In reality, it's around three steps, but the full process was shown to make it intuitive.
Once you get the hang of it, it’ll be:

  1. Identify perfect square factors(if it's a square root; if it’s a cube root, find the perfect cube),
  2. Rewrite the [radicand]{The number under the radical} as the product of the perfect square and remaining factors.
  3. Simplify by taking the square out

The essence to simplifying roots is just to find the largest square factor.

Multiplication & Division

When multiplying or dividing surds, multiply/divide the radicands] together and simplify(if possible), provided they are the same ‘power’.

Notice how when multiplying two surds, it can be condensed as one surd with the two radicands multiplied together, and when dividing two surds, it can be condensed as one surd with two radicands divided together.
